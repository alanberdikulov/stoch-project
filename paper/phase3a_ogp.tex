\section{Theory: detection threshold and forbidden-overlap interval}
\label{sec:ogp}

This section gives the project's main theoretical contribution. We work in a planted-spike
model that captures the salient features of the empirical regime identified in
\S\ref{sec:diagnostics}: heavy but sub-Gaussian tails, a strong rank-one drift driven by a
target portfolio, and isotropic Gaussian residual noise. We prove a sharp first-moment
detection threshold (Theorem~\ref{thm:detect}) and a forbidden-overlap interval
(Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid}) for the support of all near-optima. We then derive a hardness
corollary (Corollary~\ref{cor:hardness}) for any algorithm whose iterates are Hamming-stable
along an input bridge. The algorithmic content is that no such algorithm can interpolate
from a random initialization to the planted optimum without leaving the near-optimal level
set.

We close the section with an honest discussion of the OGP / classical-second-moment
structure that the simple linear model does \emph{not} possess, and explain why the
empirical OGP-style metastability observed in Phase~2b on $(n,k)=(40,10)$ should be
attributed to the full \emph{quadratic} portfolio Hamiltonian. The empirical companion in
\S\ref{sec:ogp-empirical} verifies the quantitative theory predictions on synthetic data
and reports the additional bimodality structure that emerges once the variance term is put
back in.

\subsection{Planted-spike portfolio model}

Fix integers $n,k$ with $k = \rho\, n$ for some density $\rho\in(0,1/2)$. The slice
$\Omega_{n,k} := \{\sigma\in\{0,1\}^n : \sum_{i}\sigma_i = k\}$ encodes cardinality-$k$
portfolios as binary supports. Fix a planted target $\sigma^\star\in\Omega_{n,k}$. We
assume the estimated mean-return vector takes the form
\begin{equation}
\hat\mu \;=\; \frac{\alpha}{\sqrt{k}}\,\sigma^\star \;+\; g, \qquad g\sim\mathcal N(0, I_n),
\label{eq:model}
\end{equation}
with signal-to-noise parameter $\alpha\geq 0$, and we work with the linear
score
\begin{equation}
H(\sigma) \;:=\; \hat\mu^\top\sigma \;=\; \alpha\sqrt{k}\,y(\sigma) + g^\top\sigma,
\qquad y(\sigma) := \frac{\langle\sigma,\sigma^\star\rangle}{k}\in[0,1].
\label{eq:H}
\end{equation}
Conditional on $\sigma$ (with $\sigma^\star$ fixed), $H(\sigma)\sim\mathcal N(\alpha\sqrt
k\,y(\sigma),\, k)$: the variance is independent of $\sigma$ because $\|\sigma\|_2^2=k$
for every $\sigma\in\Omega_{n,k}$.

\paragraph{Why this idealization.} Phase~1 showed that the empirical covariance is a
rank-$r^\star$ factor structure plus a near-isotropic residual; we have folded the
deterministic part of the variance term $\frac{\gamma}{2k^2}\sigma^\top\hat\Sigma\sigma$
into the planted direction $\sigma^\star$ and absorbed the residual into~$g$. The empirical
$\psi_2$-norms (median 3.6) are finite, so~\eqref{eq:model} is a Bernstein-faithful
caricature; the proofs below would carry through with sub-exponential tails at the cost of
an additive $O(\log n)$ correction in the threshold.

\paragraph{Boltzmann measure on the slice.} For $\beta>0$ we use the standard Gibbs measure
$\pi_\beta(\sigma) := \frac{1}{Z(\beta)}\,e^{\beta H(\sigma)}$ with
$Z(\beta) := \sum_{\sigma\in\Omega_{n,k}} e^{\beta H(\sigma)}$. Phase~2b validated a
swap-Metropolis chain whose stationary distribution is exactly $\pi_\beta$ (KS p-values
$\geq 0.42$ across $\beta\in\{0,50,200,1000,5000\}$).

\subsection{First-moment formula and the function $L(y;a,\rho)$}

Define, for a threshold $T\in\mathbb R$ and a planted-overlap value $y\in
\frac{1}{k}\{0,1,\dots,k\}\cap[0,1]$,
\[
A_T(y) \;:=\; \bigl\{\sigma\in\Omega_{n,k} : H(\sigma)\geq T,\ y(\sigma)=y\bigr\}.
\]
The number of $\sigma\in\Omega_{n,k}$ with planted overlap exactly $y$ is
$N_y := \binom{k}{ky}\binom{n-k}{k(1-y)}$.

\begin{lemma}[First-moment formula]\label{lem:firstmoment}
For each admissible $y$, $\mathbb E\bigl[|A_T(y)|\bigr] = N_y\cdot\overline\Phi\!\left(\frac{T-\alpha\sqrt k\,y}{\sqrt k}\right)$,
where $\overline\Phi(x)=\Pr[\mathcal N(0,1)>x]$ is the Gaussian survival function.
\end{lemma}
\begin{proof}
By linearity, $\mathbb E|A_T(y)| = \sum_{\sigma:y(\sigma)=y}\Pr[H(\sigma)\geq T]$. Each
summand equals $\overline\Phi((T-\alpha\sqrt k y)/\sqrt k)$ by~\eqref{eq:H}, and there are
$N_y$ terms.
\end{proof}

Take $\rho$ fixed, $\alpha=a\sqrt n$ with $a>0$ a constant, and threshold $T = a\sqrt{nk}$
(the expected score of the planted). Stirling and the standard Gaussian tail bound
$\overline\Phi(x)\leq e^{-x^2/2}$ give
\begin{equation}
\frac{1}{n}\log\mathbb E|A_T(y)| \;\leq\; L(y;a,\rho) + o(1),\qquad
\boxed{\ L(y;\,a,\rho) := s_\rho(y) - \tfrac{a^2}{2}(1-y)^2,\ }
\label{eq:Ldef}
\end{equation}
where the slice entropy is
\begin{equation}
s_\rho(y) \;:=\; \rho\,h(y) + (1-\rho)\,h\!\left(\tfrac{\rho(1-y)}{1-\rho}\right),
\qquad h(p) := -p\log p - (1-p)\log(1-p).
\label{eq:srho}
\end{equation}
The function $L(\,\cdot\,;a,\rho)$ is concave on $[0,1]$ with $L(1)=0$ (the planted is the
unique configuration at $y=1$, contributing entropy zero), and its sign on the interior
governs whether $T$-near-optima exist at planted-overlap~$y$.

\subsection{Detection threshold (Theorem~\ref{thm:detect})}

\begin{theorem}[Detection of the planted spike]\label{thm:detect}
Let $\rho\in(0,1/2)$ and define the \emph{detection threshold}
\begin{equation}
a_\star(\rho) \;:=\; \frac{\sqrt{2\,h(\rho)}}{1-\rho}.
\label{eq:adetect}
\end{equation}
For every $a > a_\star(\rho)$, the bulk of random subsets contributes no near-optimum:
$\mathbb P\bigl[\,\exists\,\sigma\in\Omega_{n,k}\text{ with } y(\sigma)\leq \rho + \delta
\text{ and } H(\sigma)\geq T\,\bigr]\leq e^{-c n}$ for every sufficiently small $\delta>0$
and some $c=c(a,\rho,\delta)>0$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
At $y = \rho$, $s_\rho(\rho) = h(\rho)$, so $L(\rho;a,\rho) = h(\rho) - \tfrac{a^2}{2}(1-\rho)^2 < 0$
exactly when $a > a_\star(\rho)$. By continuity of $L$ in $y$, there exists $\delta>0$ such
that $L(y;a,\rho)\leq -c'<0$ uniformly on $[0, \rho+\delta]$. Markov's inequality applied
to each of the $O(n)$ admissible discrete values of $y$ in this interval, with the
bound~\eqref{eq:Ldef}, gives the claim with $c = c'/2$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Numerical example.} For $\rho=0.20$, $a_\star(0.20) = \sqrt{2 h(0.20)}/0.8
\approx 1.250$. Below this SNR the planted spike is statistically undetectable in the
sense that exponentially many random subsets achieve the planted's expected score; above
it, no random subset does.

\subsection{Forbidden-overlap interval (Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid})}

The detection theorem only excludes near-optima from a small neighborhood of $y=\rho$. The
following sharper statement extends the forbidden region: above detection, no
$T$-near-optimum has planted-overlap below an explicit threshold $y_a\in(\rho,1)$.

\begin{theorem}[Forbidden-overlap interval]\label{thm:forbid}
Let $\rho\in(0,1/2)$, $\alpha=a\sqrt n$ with $a > a_\star(\rho)$, and $T = a\sqrt{nk}$. Define
\begin{equation}
y_a(\rho) \;:=\; \sup\bigl\{y\in[\rho,1) : L(y;a,\rho) \leq 0\bigr\}.
\label{eq:ya}
\end{equation}
Then $y_a(\rho)\in(\rho, 1)$ is well-defined, and for every sufficiently small $\delta>0$,
\begin{equation}
\Pr\bigl[\,\exists\,\sigma\in\Omega_{n,k} : \rho+\delta \leq y(\sigma) \leq y_a(\rho) - \delta
\text{ and } H(\sigma) \geq T\,\bigr] \;\leq\; e^{-c n}
\label{eq:forbid}
\end{equation}
for some $c=c(a,\rho,\delta)>0$. Equivalently, every $T$-near-optimum has planted-overlap
either below $\rho+\delta$ (excluded by Theorem~\ref{thm:detect}) or above $y_a(\rho)-\delta$.
\end{theorem}
\begin{proof}
The function $y\mapsto L(y;a,\rho)$ is continuous and concave on $[0,1]$ with $L(\rho;a,\rho)
< 0$ (since $a > a_\star(\rho)$), $L(1;a,\rho) = 0$, and $L(y;a,\rho) > 0$ for $y$ in some
interval just below $1$ because $\partial_y L(y;a,\rho)\big|_{y=1^-}$ involves the diverging
derivative $h'(0^+) = +\infty$ via the second argument of~$s_\rho$. Hence $L$ has exactly
one zero $y_a\in(\rho,1)$ in the open interval $(\rho,1)$, and $L < 0$ on $(\rho, y_a)$.
Take $c' = -\sup_{y\in[\rho+\delta, y_a-\delta]} L(y;a,\rho) > 0$ (strict positivity on a
compact subinterval where $L$ is strictly negative). Apply Markov's inequality to each of
the $O(n)$ admissible discrete values of $y$ in $[\rho+\delta, y_a-\delta]$ and sum; the
bound is dominated by $e^{-c'n} \cdot O(n)$, giving the claim with $c = c'/2$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Numerical example.} For $\rho=0.20$ and $a = 2.0$ (well above the detection
threshold $a_\star(0.20)\approx 1.25$), numerical root-finding in the companion notebook
\texttt{04a\_ogp\_theory.ipynb} gives $y_a\approx 0.55$. So no near-optimum exists with
planted overlap in $[0.21, 0.54]$; all near-optima have $y\geq 0.55$ or $y\leq 0.20$, the
latter excluded by Theorem~\ref{thm:detect}.

\subsection{Algorithmic consequence: hardness for path-stable algorithms}

We now translate the geometric statement~\eqref{eq:forbid} into a hardness theorem for any
algorithm whose iterates are Hamming-stable along an input-noise interpolation.

\begin{definition}[$\eta$-Hamming-stable along a Gaussian bridge]\label{def:stable}
Let $g_0,g_1\in\mathbb R^n$ be two i.i.d.\ realizations of the noise and define the
Gaussian bridge $g_s := \sqrt{1-s}\,g_0 + \sqrt{s}\,g_1$, $s\in[0,1]$. An algorithm
$\mathcal A:\mathbb R^n\to\Omega_{n,k}$ is \emph{$\eta$-Hamming-stable along the bridge}
if the discrete-time evolution $s\mapsto\mathcal A(\hat\mu_s)$ on the lattice
$s\in\{0,\tfrac1n,\dots,1\}$ has Hamming jump
$d_H(\mathcal A(\hat\mu_{s_-}),\mathcal A(\hat\mu_s)) \leq \eta$
between consecutive lattice points with probability $1-o(1)$.
\end{definition}

This class includes Lipschitz-then-rounded estimators (e.g.\ thresholded LASSO with smooth
sparsity tuning), low-degree polynomial estimators of the support indicator, and
constant-depth message-passing schemes. It does \emph{not} include greedy forward
selection, whose rank-$k/k+1$ boundary can swing on arbitrarily small perturbations.

\begin{corollary}[Hardness for path-stable algorithms]\label{cor:hardness}
Under the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid}, fix $\eta < k(y_a(\rho)-\rho)/2$. Suppose
the bridge endpoint $\hat\mu_0$ is such that $\mathcal A(\hat\mu_0)$ has $y(\mathcal
A(\hat\mu_0))\leq \rho + o(1)$ (e.g.\ $\mathcal A$ is invariant in distribution under
permutations of $[n]$ and the planted target is sampled uniformly). Then for every
$\eta$-Hamming-stable~$\mathcal A$ that satisfies the recovery property
$\mathcal A(\hat\mu_1)=\sigma^\star$,
\[
\Pr\bigl[\,H(\mathcal A(\hat\mu_s))\geq T \ \text{for every}\ s\in\{0,\tfrac1n,\dots,1\}\bigr]
\;\leq\; o(1) + e^{-c n}.
\]
\end{corollary}
\begin{proof}[Sketch]
By stability, $s\mapsto y(\mathcal A(\hat\mu_s))$ has discrete jumps of size at most
$\eta/k < (y_a-\rho)/2$. The endpoints satisfy $y(\mathcal A(\hat\mu_0))\leq \rho$ and
(under recovery) $y(\mathcal A(\hat\mu_1))=1$. By the discrete intermediate-value
theorem, some $s^\star\in[0,1]$ produces an output with overlap in $(\rho+\delta,
y_a(\rho)-\delta)$. By Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid} the score of any such configuration is
below $T$ with probability $1-e^{-cn}$, so $\mathcal A$ leaves the near-optimal level set
along the bridge.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{What does this exclude in our empirical setting?}
\begin{itemize}\itemsep0pt
\item LASSO-Markowitz (\S\ref{sec:lasso}) is a Lipschitz function of $\hat\mu$; rounding
to top-$k$ support introduces only $O(1)$ Hamming jumps along the bridge. Above
$a_\star(\rho)$, Corollary~\ref{cor:hardness} predicts LASSO cannot maintain near-optimality
along the input path; this matches the Phase~2a observation that LASSO's gap to the oracle
is $4$ to $11$\,bp uniformly across $(n,k)$.
\item Top-eigenvector + rounding is also $\eta$-stable with $\eta=O(1)$ outside a
measure-zero set, so the same conclusion applies.
\item Greedy forward selection is \emph{not} $\eta$-Hamming-stable along the bridge: a
single infinitesimal perturbation of $\hat\mu$ can swap two indices that were close to
the rank $k/k{+}1$ boundary and trigger a discrete combinatorial cascade. Empirically
(Phase~2a) greedy tracks the oracle closely; this is consistent with the corollary not
applying.
\item The constrained-Metropolis chain of Phase~2b is also not stable under
Definition~\ref{def:stable}: it is the very dynamics whose mixing-time blow-up at
$(40,10)$ provides empirical evidence \emph{that} the geometric barrier is real.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Discussion: classical OGP and what is conjectural}
\label{ssec:ogp-discussion}

Theorems~\ref{thm:detect} and \ref{thm:forbid} produce a forbidden-overlap interval that is
\emph{single-sided}: the forbidden region is $[\rho+\delta, y_a(\rho)-\delta]$, a single
interval terminating before the planted point. The classical OGP shape of \citep{gamarnik2017overlap}
is a forbidden interval $(y_\ell, y_h)\subsetneq(\rho,1)$ that is \emph{interior}, separating
two non-trivial allowed components. We close with three remarks.

\begin{enumerate}
\item \textbf{Why the simple model has no interior forbidden gap.} The threshold function
$a_\star(y;\rho) := \sqrt{2 s_\rho(y)}/(1-y)$ is monotonically increasing on $(\rho,1)$
(numerically verified in the companion notebook for $\rho\in[0.05, 0.40]$). Hence the
sub-level set $\{y : a_\star(y;\rho)<a\}$ is a single connected interval $[\rho, y_a(\rho)]$
for every $a>a_\star(\rho;\rho)$, ruling out a classical OGP forbidden interval from
first-moment alone.

\item \textbf{Where the classical OGP comes from in the full model.} The empirical
Hamiltonian $J(\sigma) = \tfrac{1}{k}\hat\mu^\top\sigma - \tfrac{\gamma}{2k^2}\sigma^\top
\hat\Sigma\sigma$ contains a quadratic form that is \emph{not} captured
by~\eqref{eq:model}. Phase~1 showed $\hat\Sigma$ has $32$ outlier eigenvalues, so the
quadratic form is a low-rank deterministic spike plus an isotropic random part. The
isotropic random part contributes a Sherrington-Kirkpatrick-like coupling between subsets
that, by the standard second-moment argument
(\citep{gamarnik2017overlap, ding2015proof}), is expected to produce an
interior forbidden interval at intermediate inverse temperature.

\item \textbf{Empirical verification.} The forbidden-overlap interval in
Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid} predicts that MCMC samples drawn from $\pi_\beta$ at high $\beta$
should have planted-overlap distribution concentrated either near $y=\rho$ or near $y=1$,
with no mass at intermediate overlaps. Phase~3b runs this experiment on synthetic data
matched to the empirical SNR and on the real S\&P~500 panel, and reports the bimodality
both for the linear model (where Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid} predicts it directly) and for
the full quadratic objective (where the additional interior forbidden gap is conjectural
but expected from~(2) above).
\end{enumerate}

The detection threshold of Theorem~\ref{thm:detect} and the forbidden-overlap interval
of Theorem~\ref{thm:forbid} are the rigorous theoretical contributions of the project.
The companion notebook \texttt{04a\_ogp\_theory.ipynb} verifies the threshold numerically
on a $(\rho, a)$ grid and produces Figures~\ref{fig:Lcurve},
\ref{fig:detection-curve}, and~\ref{fig:phase} of \S\ref{sec:ogp-empirical}.
